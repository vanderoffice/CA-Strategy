### A.1 E3 Position Description Template

**Purpose:** Standard job posting template for Undersecretary and Chief Deputy Director for Efficiency and Effectiveness positions.

**When to Use:** When creating or posting E3 leadership positions at agency or department level.

**Key Sections:**
- **Position Summary:** Senior leader for technology modernization, business process optimization, data governance, and operational effectiveness
- **Essential Functions:** Strategic leadership (30%), technology governance (25%), data governance (20%), performance management (15%), team leadership (10%)
- **Required Qualifications:** Two pathways—preferential hiring for Innovation Fellowship graduates OR 10+ years progressive technology leadership with 5+ years executive management
- **Authorities:** Final approval on technology investments over $5M, mandate cross-department data sharing, voting authority in Independent Data Governance Office
- **Performance Metrics:** Digital service adoption (75% by Year 5), legacy system reduction (40% by Year 5), cost savings ($50M+ annually by Year 3), citizen satisfaction (85%+)

**Related Documents:** [Section 2.2: E3 Leadership Positions](../02-governance-model.md#22-e3-leadership-positions)

### A.2 Data Sharing Agreement Template (Type 3: Moderate Sensitivity)

**Purpose:** Standardized agreement for sharing moderate-sensitivity data between state agencies and departments.

**When to Use:** When establishing data sharing arrangements for non-public data that requires privacy protections but is not subject to special regulatory requirements (e.g., HIPAA, CJIS).

**Key Sections:**
- **Purpose and Scope:** Legal authority citation, specific data elements, refresh frequency, historical depth
- **Data Elements and Specifications:** Field definitions, data quality standards (98%+ accuracy, 95%+ completeness), technical specifications (delivery method, format, encryption, authentication)
- **Security and Privacy:** Privacy Impact Assessment reference, security controls, CCPA compliance requirements, prohibited uses
- **Roles and Responsibilities:** Provider and consumer obligations, data steward contacts
- **Term and Termination:** Duration, termination conditions, post-termination data handling
- **Dispute Resolution:** Four-level escalation path from Data Stewards to Independent Data Governance Office CDO
- **Compliance and Audit:** Monitoring requirements, audit rights

**Signature Requirements:** Agency Undersecretary or delegated authority from both parties, plus Independent Office of State Data Governance approval.

**Related Documents:** [Section 3.4: Data Sharing Framework](../02-governance-model.md#34-data-sharing-framework)

### A.3 OKR Template

**Purpose:** Quarterly Objectives and Key Results tracking for statewide modernization initiatives.

**When to Use:** For setting and tracking quarterly objectives at statewide, agency, or department level.

**Key Sections:**
- **Objective Statement:** Clear, qualitative goal for the quarter
- **Key Results (3-5 per objective):** Specific, measurable outcomes with:
  - Measure definition
  - Owner assignment
  - Status tracking (In Progress / On Track / At Risk / Completed)
  - Score (0.0 - 1.0)
- **Overall Objective Score:** Average of Key Result scores
- **Lessons Learned:** What worked, what didn't, what to do differently
- **Next Quarter Implications:** How performance informs future priorities

**Example Objective:** "Establish foundational E3 governance and demonstrate early value"
- KR1: Deploy Undersecretary positions in 4 priority agencies
- KR2: Launch 10 pilot projects with 3+ showing measurable improvements
- KR3: Achieve 500 employees enrolled in Digital Accelerators program

**Related Documents:** [Appendix G.3: Change Management](09-budget-and-governance.md#g3-change-management-the-adkar-model)

### A.4 Project Stage-Gate Template

**Purpose:** Governance framework for project approval and oversight through five gates from concept to benefits realization.

**When to Use:** For all modernization projects, particularly those funded by the Breakthrough Fund or exceeding $1M.

**Gate Structure:**

| Gate | Name | Key Requirements | Approval Authority |
|------|------|------------------|-------------------|
| 0 | Concept Approval | Problem statement, E3 alignment, ROM cost estimate, executive sponsor | E3 CDD or Undersecretary |
| 1 | Feasibility | Business case with cost-benefit, stakeholder analysis, risk assessment | E3 Undersecretary (>$5M: TMO review) |
| 2 | Design Approval | Detailed design, architecture review, security/privacy review, procurement strategy | E3 Undersecretary, TMO, ARB |
| 3 | Implementation Readiness | Vendor selection, detailed plan, training plan, risk mitigation | E3 Undersecretary, TMO |
| 4 | Go-Live Approval | Testing complete, training complete, cutover plan, rollback plan | E3 Undersecretary, CDD, Director |
| 5 | Benefits Realization | Post-implementation review (90 days), benefits measurement, lessons learned | E3 Undersecretary, TMO |

**Related Documents:** [Section 4.3: Stage-Gate Governance](../02-governance-model.md#43-stage-gate-governance)

### A.5 Delegated Ordering Authority Template

**Purpose:** Formal delegation of procurement authority for task orders under Enterprise Vendor Pools.

**When to Use:** When authorizing Product Owners, E3 CDDs, or Undersecretaries to issue task orders within approved project envelopes.

**Delegation Levels:**
- **Product Owner:** Up to $500K per task order
- **E3 Chief Deputy Director:** Up to $2M per task order
- **E3 Undersecretary:** Up to $5M per task order

**Key Sections:**
- **Scope of Authority:** Delegation level, authorized framework categories (Agile Digital Services, Data & Analytics, Cloud & Platform, Cybersecurity, Change Management)
- **Limitations:** Valid only for pre-qualified pools, must comply with $15M modular cap, small business requirements
- **Training Requirements:** Modular contracting, Rapid RFI², vendor pool usage, small business requirements, AI-era pricing, ethics
- **Project Envelope:** Approved budget, period, TMO Portfolio ID
- **Oversight and Reporting:** Logging requirements, quarterly reports, performance metrics
- **Revocation Conditions:** Audit findings, non-compliance, performance failures

**Signature Requirements:** Delegated official, delegating authority, TMO acknowledgment.

**Related Documents:** [Section 9.2: Enterprise Vendor Pools](../05-procurement-guide.md#92-enterprise-vendor-pools-multi-award-msa-model)

### A.6 Standard Agile Task Order Template

**Purpose:** Standard format for issuing task orders to vendors from Enterprise Vendor Pools.

**When to Use:** For all task orders under framework contracts, whether standard on-ramp or RFI² sprint awards.

**Key Sections:**
- **Basic Information:** Task order number, framework, vendor, tier, period of performance, value, procurement type, PDL gate
- **Problem Statement and Outcomes:** Clear problem description (2-3 paragraphs), success metrics with baselines and targets
- **Scope of Work:** In scope items, out of scope items, sprint cadence
- **Team and Collaboration:** Vendor team composition, State team (Product Owner, Technical Lead, SMEs), collaboration requirements
- **Deliverables and Acceptance Criteria:** Specific deliverables with dates and measurable acceptance criteria
- **Performance Measures:** Progress reporting cadence, quality metrics (code coverage, defect rate, user acceptance)
- **AI Tool Disclosure:** Required per Section 9.10—tools to be used, quality safeguards, pricing reflection
- **Commercial Terms:** Pricing breakdown, payment terms (fixed price, T&M, or milestone-based), modification process

**Signature Requirements:** State authorized representative, vendor authorized representative.

**Related Documents:** [Section 9.10: AI-Era Pricing Expectations](../05-procurement-guide.md#910-ai-era-pricing-expectations)

### A.7 Rapid RFI² Problem Statement Template

**Purpose:** Standard format for issuing challenge-based procurement solicitations under the Rapid RFI² process.

**When to Use:** When seeking innovative solutions to well-defined problems through competitive prototyping.

**Key Sections:**
- **The Core Problem:** Current state (quantified), impact (citizens affected, staff time, cost, risk)
- **Target Metrics:** Primary, secondary, and quality metrics with current values and targets
- **Constraints:** Technical (integration, compliance, data), operational (budget, timeline, users), policy (regulatory, accessibility, language)
- **The Challenge:** Open-ended problem statement inviting innovation without prescribing solutions
- **Evaluation Criteria:** Weighted criteria for fly-off judging (user experience, technical viability, performance, scalability, innovation)
- **Timeline:** Problem statement → concept papers (2 weeks) → shortlist (+3 days) → paid prototype sprint (4-5 weeks) → fly-off (1 day) → award (+3 days)
- **Prototype Sprint Support:** Sandbox environment, sanitized data, system access, $50K prototype funding per vendor
- **Submission Requirements:** 5-page concept paper with technical architecture diagram

**Example Problem Statement:**
> "Staff spend 40% of time manually re-keying data from PDF forms. We seek a partner to prototype a solution achieving 85% auto-adjudication rate. We are not prescribing technology stack."

**Related Documents:** [Section 9.4: Rapid RFI²](../05-procurement-guide.md#94-rapid-rfi-challenge-based-procurement), [Appendix B.1: RFI² Operational Guide](07-operational-guides.md#b1-rapid-rfi-operational-guide)

### A.8 AI Productivity Disclosure Template

**Purpose:** Required disclosure for vendors using AI tools in software development, data engineering, and technology implementation.

**When to Use:** All proposals and task orders involving software development or technology implementation work.

**Key Sections:**
- **AI Tools to Be Used:** Tool name, vendor/provider, purpose, work types assisted
- **Productivity Assumptions:** Overall productivity impact estimate with justification, breakdown by work type (routine code generation, testing, documentation, complex architecture, user research)
- **Pricing Reflection:** Clear statement of how AI efficiency gains are passed through to the State—labor hours comparison, rate structure with standard vs. AI-assisted rates
- **Quality and Security Safeguards:** Code review process, security scanning (SAST, dependency scanning, AI-specific checks), technical debt management
- **Accountability Statement:** Vendor confirmation of full responsibility regardless of AI assistance

**Guidance for Low AI Benefit Claims (<10%):**
Vendors claiming minimal AI productivity benefit must explain why:
- Work is primarily non-coding
- Security/compliance restrictions prevent AI tool use
- Highly specialized domain where AI tools are not effective
- Other documented reason

**Related Documents:** [Section 9.10: AI-Era Pricing Expectations](../05-procurement-guide.md#910-ai-era-pricing-expectations)
