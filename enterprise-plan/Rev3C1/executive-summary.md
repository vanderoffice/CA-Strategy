# California Government Modernization Master Plan
## Executive Summary

**Prepared for:** Governor's Office, Office of Data and Innovation, Government Operations Agency
**Date:** November 2025
**Version:** Rev3C1 (Comprehensive Master Plan)

---

## The Challenge

California state government lacks the natural forcing mechanism that drives private sector efficiency. Without the profit motive that compels businesses to optimize or fail, government agencies accumulate technical debt, maintain outdated systems, and perpetuate inefficient processes simply because change introduces risk without consequences for inaction.

The result is measurable: legacy systems consume 70-80% of IT budgets, leaving minimal funds for innovation. Procurement cycles average 18-24 months. Citizens experience fragmented services requiring navigation of organizational charts rather than seamless support for life events. The average critical government system is over 25 years old.

California cannot wait for organic improvement. The state must deliberately create the forcing mechanism that drives efficiency and effectiveness through institutional design.

---

## The Solution: The E3 Operational Model

The **Enterprise Efficiency and Effectiveness (E3) Operational Model** creates institutional accountability for modernization through explicit leadership positions with authority over technology, process, and organizational transformation.

### Three Integrated Components

**1. Institutional Accountability (The Engine)**

New leadership positions create the forcing mechanism government inherently lacks:

- **Undersecretaries for Efficiency and Effectiveness** at the Agency level with authority over technology modernization, business process optimization, data governance, and performance management
- **Chief Deputy Directors for Efficiency and Effectiveness** at the Department level executing operational transformation and managing project portfolios
- **Transformation Management Office** coordinating efforts statewide, managing the project portfolio, tracking performance, and providing change management support

**2. Product-Led Delivery (The Method)**

Replace project-centric "waterfall" approaches with continuous product delivery:

- **Life Experience Teams** organized around citizen journeys (having a baby, starting a business, disaster recovery) rather than agency boundaries
- **Service Mesh Architecture** connecting systems via APIs instead of monolithic centralized databases
- **Modular Contracting** with a $10 million cap on IT contracts to reduce vendor lock-in and enable smaller, innovative firms to compete
- **RFI² Procurement** (Request for Innovative Ideas) defining problems and soliciting solutions rather than prescribing specific technologies

**3. Data Governance Foundation (The Catalyst)**

**Recommended Approach: Option B - Independent Office of State Data Governance**

Establish a cross-branch, independent Office of State Data Governance modeled on California's Little Hoover Commission:

- **Board Composition:** Appointments from Governor, Legislature, Judicial Council, and Constitutional Officers ensuring cross-branch legitimacy
- **Authority:** Set statewide data standards, approve sharing frameworks, convene all branches for policy development
- **Durability:** Survives administration transitions with staggered 3-year board terms
- **Actual Power:** E3 leaders gain enforceable authority over data sharing and standardization, not merely advisory roles

This independent structure provides the E3 model with actual authority over efficiency and effectiveness, not just coordination responsibilities.

## Talent Pipeline and Sustainability

### Governor's Innovation Fellowship

A continuous talent pipeline feeds the E3 structure:

- **6-month cohorts** of 21 fellows launched every 6 months
- **Department embedding** for hands-on experience outside home agencies
- **Group initiatives** delivering cross-agency modernization solutions
- **Post-fellowship engagement** as on-call subject matter experts and emergency response capacity
- **Preferential hiring** into E3 and other executive positions, with fellowship completion as desirable qualification in duty statements

By the start of 2027, three complete cohorts provide 63 trained modernization leaders actively engaged across state government.

### Data Governance Teams

Permanent state staff teams providing sustainable capacity:

- **Blended expertise model** integrating domain specialists (Environmental Scientists, Engineers, Engineering Geologists) with dedicated data professionals (Research Data Specialists at senior/supervisory levels)
- **Professional accountability** through PE/PG licensure where applicable and supervisory classification standards
- **Scalable structure** adaptable to any department using equivalent classification series
- **Team composition** ranging from 8-10 FTE for small departments to 20-25 FTE for large departments

These teams support Fellows' projects and sustain innovations after Fellows complete their assignments.

## Breakthrough Funding Model

Replace the proposed Technology Working Capital Fund with the **California Breakthrough Modernization Fund**:

**Seed Investment Phase:**
- California Breakthrough Project partners provide catalytic capital alongside state appropriation
- Creates "Innovation Sidecar Fund" co-investing with state dollars
- Accelerates ability to take on higher-risk, high-upside modernization projects

**Feedback Loop:**
- Projects demonstrate efficiency gains and cost savings
- Department baseline budgets reduced to reflect realized efficiencies
- Fixed portion (25-40%) of released funding diverted into Breakthrough Fund
- Remainder returns to General Fund, providing both fiscal benefit and replenished modernization pool

**Portfolio Reporting:**
- ODI manages fund using private-sector portfolio approach
- Annual "Modernization Portfolio Report" analogous to fund letter showing deployed capital, realized savings, key wins, and reusable components
- Breakthrough partners receive granular dashboards emphasizing co-learning and replication, not governance control

This creates a self-reinforcing cycle where each successful modernization generates capacity for the next.

## Implementation Horizons

### 6-Month Horizon: Foundation

**Establish Governance and Authority**
- Launch legislative process for Independent Office of State Data Governance
- Appoint first cohort of Undersecretaries (4 priority agencies)
- Establish Transformation Management Office with interim leadership
- Launch Governor's Innovation Fellowship Cohort 1 (21 fellows, January 2026)
- Charter first three Life Experience Teams (pilot projects)

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Legislation introduced and moving through committee process
- E3 leadership positions filled and operational
- TMO providing portfolio coordination
- Fellows embedded in departments beginning modernization work
- 3-5 quick wins demonstrating capability and building momentum

### 12-Month Horizon: Launch and Scale

**Operationalize Core Capabilities**
- Complete Independent Office board appointments and hire Executive Director/Chief Data Officer
- Deploy Chief Deputy Directors across 10 highest-impact departments
- Graduate Fellowship Cohort 1 and launch Cohort 2
- Publish statewide data standards in at least one sector
- Seed Breakthrough Modernization Fund and launch first wave of projects
- Establish data governance teams in 3-5 pilot departments

**Expected Outcomes:**
- Independent Office operational with initial policy framework
- 10 departments with operational E3 execution capability
- 42 total Fellows (21 graduated, 21 in Cohort 2)
- Demonstrated data sharing improvements
- $50M+ in documented cost savings from early initiatives
- 5-10 visible service improvements

### 36-Month Horizon: Transformation

**Achieve Measurable Impact**
- Complete E3 deployment across all major agencies (8-12 Undersecretaries) and departments (30-40 Chief Deputy Directors)
- Graduate 6 Fellowship cohorts (126 trained modernization leaders)
- Establish data governance teams statewide
- 50% digital adoption for high-volume services
- 25% legacy system reduction
- $250M+ in documented cost savings and cost avoidance
- Breakthrough Fund self-sustaining through efficiency reinvestment

**Expected Outcomes:**
- E3 model embedded in state government operations
- Continuous pipeline of trained change agents
- Measurable citizen satisfaction improvements
- Working interoperability between previously siloed systems
- California positioned as national model for government modernization

## Critical Success Factors

**Executive Commitment**
Sustained engagement from Governor and Agency Secretaries for 5+ years. Government transformation requires 7-10 years on average; early leadership departure derails momentum.

**Actual Authority**
E3 leaders must have real decision-making power over technology investments, data sharing mandates, and performance accountability—not merely advisory roles. The Independent Office model provides this authority.

**Adequate Funding**
Clear ROI projections justify investment, but appropriations must align with ambition. The Breakthrough Fund provides initial catalytic capital and creates sustainable reinvestment.

**Cultural Transformation**
Technology represents 30% of transformation; people and process account for 70%. Change management investment must match technical investment.

**Quick Wins**
Deliver 5-10 visible improvements within Year 1 to build credibility, demonstrate capability, and overcome skepticism.

**Measurement Discipline**
Transparent reporting of progress and setbacks. Publish metrics even when unfavorable. What gets measured gets managed.

**Talent Development**
The Fellowship creates a pipeline, but sustained capability requires workforce investment at scale through data governance teams and training programs.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| **Short timeline for Independent Office** | HIGH | Early stakeholder engagement, concurrent legislative and planning work, interim steering committee if needed |
| **Resistance to change** | HIGH | Innovation Ambassadors, protected learning time, no-blame experimentation zones, union engagement, quick wins demonstrating value |
| **Budget constraints** | HIGH | Breakthrough seed capital, phased approach allowing adjustment, early ROI demonstration, federal grant pursuit |
| **Political transitions** | MEDIUM | Cross-branch Independent Office structure, bipartisan framing, embedded governance, early wins demonstrating value |
| **Vendor dependencies** | MEDIUM | Open standards requirements, API-first architecture, modular contracting cap, competitive procurement |
| **Fellowship talent retention** | MEDIUM | Preferential hiring pathways, executive duty statement integration, ongoing SME network engagement, competitive advancement opportunities |

## California as National Model

This Master Plan positions California as the definitive government modernization model for other U.S. states. By documenting the E3 Operational Model, Independent Data Governance Office, Fellowship program, Breakthrough funding model, and implementation playbooks, California enables peer states to adopt proven approaches.

**Key Differentiators:**
- **Forcing Mechanism:** E3 model creates efficiency driver government inherently lacks
- **Actual Authority:** Independent Office provides cross-branch legitimacy and enforceable power
- **Talent Pipeline:** Continuous Fellowship cohorts provide sustained change agent supply
- **Sustainable Funding:** Breakthrough model converts efficiency gains into ongoing modernization capacity
- **Product-Led Delivery:** Life Experience Teams and modular contracting break traditional project cycles
- **Evidence-Based:** Comprehensive performance framework enables rigorous evaluation

California's scale (40 million residents, $400B+ budget, 170 departments, 230,000+ employees) demonstrates applicability to states of all sizes.

## Conclusion

California's government modernization challenge is fundamentally an organizational design problem, not merely a technology problem. The E3 Operational Model creates the forcing mechanism for efficiency that government inherently lacks, ensuring sustained focus on effectiveness and continuous improvement.

By establishing the Independent Office of State Data Governance with actual cross-branch authority, launching the Governor's Innovation Fellowship as a continuous talent pipeline, deploying professional data governance teams for sustainable capacity, and creating the Breakthrough Modernization Fund for catalytic investment and reinvestment, California establishes a comprehensive, replicable approach to government transformation.

Success requires executive commitment, actual authority structures, adequate resources, cultural transformation, and measurement discipline. The Master Plan provides the roadmap; execution determines whether California realizes its potential as the national model for 21st-century government effectiveness.

**The question is not whether California can afford to modernize—it's whether California can afford not to.**
