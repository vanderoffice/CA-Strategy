# California State Government Modernization Strategy
## Presentation Deck Outline

---

## Slide Structure & Content Guide

This outline provides the complete structure for a presentation deck on California's modernization strategy. Each slide includes title, key messages, suggested visuals, and speaker notes guidance.

---

### **Slide 1: Title Slide**

**Title:** California State Government Modernization Strategy  
**Subtitle:** The Enterprise Efficiency and Effectiveness (E3) Operational Model

**Visual Elements:**
- California state seal or outline
- Modern, clean design aesthetic
- Date and classification (if applicable)

**Footer:** Prepared for Office of Data and Innovation & Governor's Office of Operations

### **Slide 2: Executive Summary**

**Title:** Transforming California Government for the 21st Century

**Key Messages:**
- California government lacks the profit-driven forcing mechanism that drives private sector efficiency
- The E3 Operational Model creates institutional accountability through new leadership positions
- Integration with Governor's Innovation Fellowship and California Breakthrough Project accelerates delivery
- Expected outcomes: 75% digital service adoption, 85%+ citizen satisfaction, $250M+ cost savings by Year 3

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Four-quadrant graphic showing: Problem → Solution → Approach → Outcomes
- Icons representing citizens, technology, leadership, and innovation

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Start with the core problem: government efficiency without profit motive
- Connect to citizen frustration with current service delivery
- Position E3 as structural solution, not just technology initiative
- Emphasize California as national model for other states

---

### **Slide 3: The Efficiency Challenge**

**Title:** Government Without a Profit Motive: Why California Must Create a Forcing Mechanism

**Key Messages:**
- Private sector: Poor efficiency → lost revenue → business failure (forcing mechanism exists)
- Government: Budget allocations continue regardless of efficiency (no inherent pressure)
- Consequences: 70-80% of IT budget on legacy systems, 18-24 month procurement cycles, outdated infrastructure
- California must deliberately create the forcing mechanism through institutional design

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Side-by-side comparison: Private Sector vs. Government incentives
- Graph showing IT budget allocation (80% legacy maintenance vs. 20% innovation)
- Timeline showing typical government procurement cycle (18-24 months)

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Acknowledge this is uncomfortable truth about government operations
- Not a criticism of employees—systemic problem requiring systemic solution
- Use specific examples: DMV wait times, redundant forms, outdated systems
- Frame as opportunity, not blame

---

### **Slide 4: The E3 Operational Model**

**Title:** Enterprise Efficiency and Effectiveness (E3): Creating Institutional Accountability

**Key Messages:**
- New Undersecretary positions at Governor's Office/Agency level (statewide coordination)
- Chief Deputy Director positions at department level (operational execution)
- Explicit authority over technology, data, procurement, and performance
- Staffed preferentially from Governor's Innovation Fellowship (proven delivery capability)

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Reference to original E3 architecture diagram from idea board
- Organizational chart showing governance structure
- Flow showing Fellowship → E3 Position career pathway

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Walk through architecture diagram highlighting key components
- Explain how dual-track structure ensures both strategic oversight and tactical execution
- Emphasize preferential hiring from Fellowship creates merit-based talent pipeline
- Connect to California Breakthrough Project advisory role

---

### **Slide 5: Governance Structure**

**Title:** Multi-Layered Governance Ensuring Accountability

**Key Messages:**
- Statewide E3 Council coordinates across agencies (Undersecretaries + GovOps)
- Executive Technology Council provides strategic direction (Agency Secretaries)
- Technical Architecture Review Board ensures standards compliance
- Transformation Management Office (TMO) provides centralized support and tracking
- California Breakthrough Project brings Silicon Valley expertise

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Layered diagram showing governance tiers (Strategic → Coordination → Operational → Support)
- Logos/photos of California Breakthrough Project partners (Ron Conway, Chris Larsen, Jeff Lawson, Jen Pahlka)

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Emphasize this is not more bureaucracy—each layer has specific purpose
- TMO reduces burden on agencies by providing shared services
- Breakthrough Project provides world-class private sector perspective
- Governance enables coordination while preserving agency autonomy

---

### **Slide 6: Technology Architecture Highlights**

**Title:** Modern, Interoperable, Secure Infrastructure

**Key Messages:**
- Enterprise Service Bus (ESB) enables system integration (hub-and-spoke vs. spaghetti)
- API-first architecture with centralized gateway and developer portal
- Legacy system modernization using strangler pattern (incremental replacement)
- Cloud-native infrastructure and Zero Trust security

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Before/After diagram: Point-to-point integrations → ESB hub-and-spoke
- Technology stack visual (ESB, API Gateway, Cloud, Security layers)
- Legacy modernization timeline showing gradual replacement

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Use simple language; avoid technical jargon for executive audience
- Focus on business benefits: faster integration, lower cost, better security
- Explain strangler pattern prevents "big bang" failures
- UK Government reduced integration time 60% using similar approach

---

### **Slide 7: Data Governance Framework**

**Title:** Statewide Data Governance Enabling Collaboration

**Key Messages:**
- Three-tier data governance (Statewide CDO Council → Agency Teams → Department Stewards)
- Standardized data sharing agreement templates reduce negotiation from 18 months to 30 days
- Master Data Management (MDM) creates "golden records" for citizens, businesses, locations
- Privacy and ethics framework ensures CCPA compliance and responsible AI use

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Three-tier pyramid showing governance structure
- Timeline showing agreement negotiation before (18 months) vs. after (30 days) templates
- Circular diagram showing MDM benefits (data quality, reduced redundancy, faster integration)

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Data sharing currently major barrier to integrated services
- Template approach proven successful in other states (Connecticut 45% silo reduction)
- MDM prevents "same person, different ID in every system" problem
- Privacy protection is requirement, not afterthought

---

### **Slide 8: Procurement Modernization - The RFI² Innovation**

**Title:** Request for Innovative Ideas (RFI²): California's Procurement Game-Changer

**Key Messages:**
- California pioneered RFI² under Executive Order N-04-19 (2019)
- Two-phase process: Innovation Concept Papers → Proof of Concept → Implementation
- Asks vendors to design solutions, not just bid on predefined specs
- Success examples: Wildfire response (131 proposals), Middle-Mile Broadband (83% of network), GenAI bill analysis

**Suggested Visuals:**
- RFI² process flow diagram (Phase 1 → Innovation Conference → PoC → Contract Award)
- Map showing Middle-Mile Broadband network coverage
- Photos from Innovation Conference (vendors collaborating)

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Explain traditional procurement defines solution upfront (limits innovation)
- RFI² flips model: define problem, let market innovate
- Vendor compensation for concept papers ensures quality participation
- Ideal for complex, high-uncertainty challenges where best solution unknown
- Position as California innovation other states can adopt

---

### **Slide 9: Workforce Strategy**

**Title:** Developing Talent for Modern Government

**Key Messages:**
- Digital Accelerators Program: Training all IT staff in cloud, AI/ML, agile, cybersecurity
- Governor's Innovation Fellowship: 6-month delivery program with preferential E3 hiring
- Protected learning time: 20% of work hours dedicated to skill development
- Competitive compensation addressing private sector gaps (20-40%)

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Training curriculum visual showing six tracks (Cloud, DevOps, AI/ML, Data, Security, Agile)
- Innovation Fellowship cohort timeline (new cohort every 4 months, overlapping)
- Skills gap chart showing current state vs. target state

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Acknowledge talent is California's biggest challenge
- Cannot compete on salary alone, but total package (pension, mission, work-life balance) is competitive
- Fellowship creates "try before you buy" talent pipeline
- 33% of government workers have low digital skills; training is non-negotiable
- GDIT trained 20,000+ employees successfully—California can too

### **Slide 10: Performance Measurement Approach**

**Title:** Rigorous Measurement and Transparent Accountability

**Key Messages:**
- Four-tier KPI framework: Strategic (citizen-facing) → Operational (technical) → Transformation (program health) → Organizational (culture)
- OKR (Objectives & Key Results) quarterly focus aligned with Envision 2026
- Public dashboard showing real-time progress (transparency builds trust)
- Quarterly executive reports to Governor and Legislature

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Four-tier pyramid showing KPI structure
- Sample dashboard mockup (digital adoption %, CSAT score, legacy reduction %)
- OKR example showing objective with 3 key results

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- What gets measured gets managed—discipline is critical
- Public transparency prevents window-dressing and creates accountability
- OKRs provide quarterly focus preventing drift
- Learn from both successes and failures (report honestly)

### **Slide 11: Phased Implementation Roadmap**

**Title:** Disciplined, Phased Approach Delivering Continuous Value

**Key Messages:**
- **Phase 1 - Foundation:** Establish governance, assess current state, launch 10 pilots, demonstrate early wins
- **Phase 2 - Scale:** Deploy ESB/APIs, expand E3 leadership, execute 25% legacy migration, 50% digital adoption
- **Phase 3 - Transformation:** Full platform deployment, 50% legacy reduction, 75% digital adoption, $250M+ ROI
- **Phase 4 - Sustainability:** Complete modernization, embed in culture, document playbooks, national leadership

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Four-phase timeline with key milestones and outcomes
- Gantt chart showing overlapping phases (Phase 2 begins before Phase 1 complete)
- Success metrics by phase

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Transformation takes 7-10 years on average—must set realistic expectations
- Phased approach enables learning and course correction
- Early wins in Phase 1 critical for building momentum and credibility
- Each phase builds on previous, not discrete initiatives

---

### **Slide 12: Quick Wins Strategy**

**Title:** Delivering Visible Improvements in First 90 Days

**Key Messages:**
- 5-7 "quick win" projects: 90-day delivery, visible impact, low complexity
- Examples: Digitize paper forms, deploy chatbot, automate reports, e-signatures
- Goals: Build credibility, demonstrate capability, overcome skepticism, generate momentum
- Pair with 3-5 strategic pilots (longer timeline, foundational capabilities)

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Icons representing quick win examples (mobile phone for digital forms, robot for chatbot, etc.)
- Before/After comparison showing process improvement
- Timeline showing 90-day quick win delivery vs. 12-18 month strategic pilot

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Government IT has reputation for long timelines and failures
- Quick wins prove E3 model can deliver rapidly
- Choose projects with high visibility to citizens or frontline staff
- Success breeds success—early wins create coalition for larger changes

### **Slide 13: Success Factors and Risks**

**Title:** What Will Determine Success or Failure

**Success Factors:**
- Sustained executive commitment (5+ years, Governor and Agency Secretaries)
- Adequate funding aligned with ambition
- Cultural transformation investment (70% of challenge is people/process, 30% technology)
- Measurement discipline and transparent reporting
- Partnership with California Breakthrough Project, private sector, academia

**Key Risks:**
- Resistance to change from established workforce (mitigation: Innovation Ambassadors, training, no-layoff guarantee)
- Budget constraints limiting scope (mitigation: phased approach, demonstrate ROI early)
- Political transitions disrupting momentum (mitigation: bipartisan framing, embedded governance)
- Cybersecurity incidents during migration (mitigation: Zero Trust, testing, incident response)

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Two-column table: Success Factors | Risk Mitigation Strategies
- Risk heat map showing probability vs. impact

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Be honest about challenges—this is difficult work
- Most government IT transformations fail due to underestimating cultural resistance
- Success requires sustained leadership commitment through political cycles
- Risk mitigation strategies reduce but don't eliminate risks—manage actively

**Suggested Visuals:**
- Budget curve showing spending by year (ramp up, peak, decline to steady state)

**Speaker Notes Guidance:**
- Cannot modernize on declining budgets—requires investment
- Front-load spending; savings materialize in Years 3-5
- Leadership positions are permanent; Fellowship provides rotating talent
- Benchmark against other states: California's scale requires proportional investment

### **Presentation Delivery Recommendations**

**Audience Adaptation:**

**For Governor/Legislature (15-20 minutes):**
- Use slides 1-4, 8, 11-14, 16 (focus on problem, solution, ROI, next steps)
- Heavy emphasis on citizen impact and cost savings
- Minimal technical detail

**For Agency Secretaries/Department Directors (30-40 minutes):**
- Use all slides except 17
- Moderate technical detail
- Emphasize governance, workforce, and change management (their responsibilities)

**For Technical Implementation Teams (60+ minutes):**
- Use all slides
- Add technical appendix slides with architecture diagrams, API specifications, security controls
- Deep dive into implementation approach

**Visual Design Principles:**
- Clean, modern aesthetic aligned with California Digital Strategy branding
- Consistent color scheme (California blue/gold)
- Minimal text per slide (visual storytelling)
- High-quality photos and graphics (avoid clip art)
- Accessibility compliant (high contrast, readable fonts)

**Next Steps After Presentation:**
- Leave-behind: Executive Brief (4 pages) and one-page summary
- Offer follow-up meetings for deeper dives on specific topics
- Provide contact information for strategy team
- Schedule decision-making session within 2 weeks of presentation
