## Appendix A: Conflicts and Redundancies

This section documents conflicts and redundancies identified during synthesis of source documents, flagged for human review and decision-making.

### A.1 Major Conflicts

**CONFLICT 1: Data Governance Structure (Option A vs. Option B)**

**Source Documents:**
- Rev2G3: Presents two options (A and B) with detailed implementation for each
- Rev3G3 Executive Brief: Recommends "Option A: Statutory Interagency Governance"
- Instructions: Specify "Use Option B (independent office), not Option A"

**Conflict Description:**

**Option A (Rev3G3 Recommendation):**
- Strengthens existing interagency governance
- Statutory California Interagency Data Council (CIDC)
- Works through existing ODI and GovOps structures
- Faster to implement (no new entity creation)
- Lower upfront cost

**Option B (Instructions Specified):**
- Independent Office of State Data Governance
- Cross-branch board (Governor, Legislature, Judicial, Constitutional Officers)
- New statutory entity separate from executive branch
- Longer implementation timeline (requires legislation, board appointments, new infrastructure)
- Higher upfront cost but greater long-term durability

**Resolution Adopted in Master Plan:**
This plan adopts **Option B** per instructions, as it provides:
1. Actual authority over efficiency and effectiveness (not merely coordination)
2. Cross-branch legitimacy ensuring buy-in beyond executive branch
3. Durability across administration transitions
4. Enforceable power for E3 leaders to mandate data sharing and standardization

**Recommendation:** Executive leadership should confirm Option B selection, as it represents significant institutional commitment and legislative effort.

---

**CONFLICT 2: Technology Working Capital Fund vs. Breakthrough Modernization Fund**

**Source Documents:**
- Original and Rev1G3: Propose "Technology Working Capital Fund" as revolving fund
- Breakthrough-seeding.md: Proposes "Future Technology Modernization Fund" (FTMF) with Breakthrough partner seed investment

**Conflict Description:**

**Technology Working Capital Fund:**
- Traditional revolving fund model
- Agencies borrow for modernization, repay from savings
- Funded by state appropriations
- Familiar government financing mechanism

**Breakthrough Modernization Fund:**
- Seed investment from California Breakthrough Project partners
- Portfolio management approach (private-sector style)
- Efficiency savings diverted back to fund (25-40% portion)
- Investment-style reporting to partners
- Creates feedback loop and co-learning model

**Resolution Adopted in Master Plan:**
This plan adopts **Breakthrough Modernization Fund** as it provides:
1. Catalytic capital accelerating early projects
2. Private-sector accountability and reporting models
3. Self-reinforcing feedback loop
4. Partnership engagement for innovation and best practices

**Recommendation:** Confirm California Breakthrough Project partner commitments and seed investment amounts before finalizing fund structure.

**REDUNDANCY 2: Data Governance Council Structures**

**Source Documents:**
Multiple overlapping council/committee structures mentioned:
- Statewide Data Governance Council
- California Interagency Data Council (CIDC)
- Agency Data Governance Teams
- CDO Council
- Independent Data Office Board

**Clarification Provided in Master Plan:**

With adoption of Option B (Independent Office), structure is:

**Independent Office Board (Cross-branch governance body):**
- Sets policy, appoints CDO, approves standards
- Quarterly meetings
- Final authority on statewide standards

**Statewide Data Governance Council (Technical coordination):**
- Chaired by Independent Office CDO
- Members: Agency Data Officers
- Monthly meetings implementing board policies
- Technical standards development

**Agency Data Governance Teams:**
- Chaired by Agency Data Officer
- Members: Department Data Stewards
- Bi-weekly meetings
- Agency-specific implementation

**Department Data Operations:**
- Led by Department Data Steward
- Operational implementation
- Weekly meetings

**Recommendation:** Publish organizational chart showing all governance bodies, their relationships, authorities, and meeting cadences.

Multiple training programs mentioned without clear distinction:

- CalAcademy

- Target: Practitioners in specific domains (data stewards, analysts, etc.)

- Facilitation: TMO coordination

### A.3 Minor Conflicts and Inconsistencies

**Issue: Timeline for Independent Office Launch**

**Conflict:**
- Rev2G3 Option B: Suggests 13-month timeline (Nov 2025 - Jan 2027) assuming immediate legislative action
- Rev3G3: Assumes faster implementation timeline
- Reality: Governor Newsom's term ends January 2027, creating urgency

**Resolution:**
Master Plan adopts aggressive but realistic timeline:
- Months 1-2: Draft legislation and stakeholder alignment
- Months 3-6: Legislative process and passage
- Months 7-9: Board appointments and Executive Director search
- Months 10-12: Office operational with initial standards

**Risk:** Timeline is compressed. Legislative delays or resistance could push operational date beyond current administration.

**Recommendation:** Begin legislative drafting and stakeholder engagement immediately. Consider interim steering committee if legislation delayed.

**Conflict:**
- Original: Suggests 8-12 Undersecretary positions
- Rev3G3: References 4 initial agencies
- Various documents: Inconsistent total counts

**Resolution:**
Master Plan adopts phased approach:
- Phase 1: 4 positions (GovOps, CalHHS, Transportation, Corrections)
- Phase 2: 4 additional (8 total)
- Phase 3: Complete deployment (8-12 total depending on agency structure)

**Recommendation:** Finalize exact agency list and deployment sequence based on modernization priorities and agency readiness.

**Issue: Fellowship Cohort Size**

**Conflict:**
- Fellowship_Plan_Part1.md: Specifies 21 fellows per cohort
- Other references: Vary between 15-25

**Resolution:**
Master Plan adopts **21 fellows per cohort** as specified in detailed Fellowship plan.

## Appendix B: Classification Standards for Data Governance Teams

This section provides detailed civil service classification information for establishing data governance teams.

### B.1 Environmental Scientist Series

**Environmental Scientist (Range A/B/C)**

**Range A (Entry Level):**
- Salary: $5,597 - $7,004 per month
- Supervision: Close supervision
- Work: Less difficult scientific work
- Typical duties: Data collection, field sampling, basic analysis

**Range B (Intermediate Working Level):**
- Salary: $6,270 - $7,850 per month
- Supervision: General supervision
- Work: Average difficulty professional work
- Typical duties: Independent investigations, report preparation, technical guidance

**Range C (Full Journey Level):**
- Salary: $7,004 - $8,770 per month
- Supervision: Direction (not close supervision)
- Work: Difficult professional scientific work
- May act as project managers or lead capacity over other staff
- Typical duties: Complex investigations, policy development, lead technical work

**Senior Environmental Scientist**

Two tracks available:

**Specialist Track:**
- Salary: $8,210 - $10,266 per month (approximately $98,500 - $123,000 annually)
- Advanced journey level
- Most complex technical assignments
- Independently identifies problems and develops courses of action
- Prepares guidance, policy, and regulatory documents
- Serves as consultant to management and other agencies
- No supervisory responsibility

**Supervisory Track:**
- Salary: $8,210 - $10,266 per month
- First supervisory level
- Directs professional and technical staff
- Authority for staff development, performance evaluation, program budgeting
- Represents organization to external agencies
- Coordinates program activities

**Environmental Program Manager I/II**

**EPM I:**
- Salary: $9,679 - $12,009 per month (approximately $116,000 - $144,000 annually)
- Management oversight responsibility
- Formulates and administers policies and programs
- Coordinates technical and administrative sections
- Represents organization in compliance negotiations
- Strategic planning authority

**EPM II:**
- Salary: $10,828 - $13,452 per month (approximately $130,000 - $161,000 annually)
- Higher-level management
- Broader scope and complexity
- Multi-program coordination
- Executive-level representation

### B.2 Engineer Series

**Associate Civil Engineer**

- Salary: $9,754 - $12,208 per month (approximately $117,000 - $146,500 annually)
- Plans and directs design and preparation of drawings, specifications, estimates
- Performs difficult engineering office and field work
- Requirements: Bachelor's degree in civil engineering plus experience
- Professional Engineer (PE) license preferred but not required at this level

**Senior Civil Engineer**

- Salary: $10,311 - $12,905 per month (approximately $124,000 - $155,000 annually)
- Plans work and directs staff of engineers
- Passes on difficult problems and determines methods
- Supervises preparation of maps, plans, charts
- Makes specialized technical studies
- **Requirements:** Valid Certificate of Registration as Professional Engineer (PE) issued by California Board, plus five years civil engineering experience
- **Special Personal Characteristic:** Demonstrated supervisory ability

**PE Licensure Value:**
- Requires passing Fundamentals of Engineering (FE) exam
- Requires 4+ years of progressive engineering experience
- Requires passing Principles and Practice of Engineering (PE) exam
- Professional responsibility for work product accuracy
- "Responsible charge" accountability
- Continuing education requirements

### B.3 Engineering Geologist Series

**Engineering Geologist (Range A/B/C/D)**

**Range A (Entry):**
- Salary: $5,597 - $7,004 per month
- Close supervision
- Less difficult engineering geologic work

**Range B (Intermediate):**
- Salary: $6,270 - $7,850 per month
- General supervision
- Average difficulty work

**Range C (Full Journey, Non-Registered):**
- Salary: $7,004 - $8,770 per month
- Direction (not close supervision)
- Difficult professional work
- May act as project managers or staff specialists
- May serve in lead capacity

**Range D (Full Journey, Registered):**
- Salary: $7,849 - $9,826 per month
- **Requires:** Valid certificate of registration as geologist or geophysicist issued by California Board of Geologists and Geophysicists
- **Act in responsible charge capacity**
- Professional accountability for geologic determinations

**Senior Engineering Geologist**

- Salary: $10,311 - $12,905 per month (approximately $124,000 - $155,000 annually)
- Either first-line unit supervisor OR nonsupervisory staff specialist
- Most complex and technical engineering geologic assignments
- Requirements: Registration as geologist plus experience
- Desirable: Certification as engineering geologist or hydrogeologist

**Supervising Engineering Geologist**

- Salary: $11,538 - $14,439 per month (approximately $138,500 - $173,000 annually)
- Full supervisory level
- Supervises two or more sections through subordinate supervisors
- Plans, organizes, and directs geological work
- Coordinates with other organizational components

## Appendix D: Technology Standards Catalog

### D.1 API Standards

**Required Standards (All APIs):**
- RESTful design following HTTP semantics
- JSON as primary data format (XML supported for legacy compatibility)
- HTTPS only (no unencrypted HTTP)
- Versioning in URL path (e.g., /api/v2/permits)
- Pagination for list endpoints (maximum 100 items per page)
- Standard error responses with HTTP status codes and messages
- OpenAPI 3.0 specification for all APIs

**Authentication and Authorization:**
- OAuth 2.0 for external consumers (citizens, businesses, third-party developers)
- Mutual TLS for high-security government-to-government APIs
- API keys with rotation requirements (maximum 90-day validity)
- Role-based access control (RBAC) aligned with data sensitivity

**Data Protection:**
- TLS 1.3 encryption for all API traffic
- PII redaction in logs and error messages
- Rate limiting to prevent abuse (varies by API classification)
- IP whitelisting for high-sensitivity APIs

**Performance Requirements:**
- < 200ms average response time (95th percentile)
- < 500ms maximum response time (99th percentile)
- 99.95% availability for production APIs
- Graceful degradation under load

### D.2 Data Exchange Standards

**Health and Human Services:**
- HL7 FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) for health data exchange
- ICD-10 for diagnosis coding
- CPT for treatment and service coding
- LOINC for laboratory observations

**Justice and Corrections:**
- NIEM (National Information Exchange Model) for criminal justice data
- UCR (Uniform Crime Reporting) for crime statistics
- CJIS (Criminal Justice Information Services) standards for security

**Education:**
- CEDS (Common Education Data Standards) for enrollment, graduation, assessment
- SIF (Schools Interoperability Framework) for student information systems

**Geospatial:**
- OGC Standards (Open Geospatial Consortium) for GIS interoperability
- USNG (United States National Grid) for location referencing

### D.3 Cybersecurity Standards

**Framework:**
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover)
- NIST 800-53 security and privacy controls
- California SIMM 5300 requirements

**Specific Controls:**
- DISA STIGs for configuration standards
- Zero Trust Architecture principles
- Encryption: TLS 1.3 (transit), AES-256 (at rest)
- Multi-factor authentication for all administrative access
- Security logging and monitoring (SIEM)
- Regular vulnerability scanning and penetration testing
- Incident response plan and tabletop exercises

### D.4 Cloud Standards

**Approved Cloud Providers:**
- AWS GovCloud
- Microsoft Azure Government
- Google Cloud Platform (with appropriate certifications)

**Required Certifications:**
- FedRAMP Authorized (Moderate or High depending on data sensitivity)
- FISMA compliance
- StateRAMP (California-specific requirements)

**Architecture Principles:**
- Cloud-native design (containers, serverless, managed services)
- Multi-cloud strategy to avoid vendor lock-in
- Infrastructure as Code (IaC) using Terraform or CloudFormation
- Automated backup and disaster recovery
- Cost optimization and monitoring
